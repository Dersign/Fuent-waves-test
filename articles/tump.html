    Trump Advises Putin to Avoid Escalating Ukraine War

    10 Nov, 2024

    Donald Trump, the US president-elect, held a phone call with Russian President Vladimir Putin on Thursday 7th Nov' 2024 to discuss the ongoing war in Ukraine. According to reports from The Washington Post, Trump urged Putin not to escalate the conflict further and reminded him of the United States' strong military presence in Europe.


    Trump also expressed his hope for a peaceful resolution to the war and indicated that he would be interested in further discussions to help bring about an end to the fighting. During his campaign for the presidency, Trump had stated that he could resolve the war "within a day", although he did not specify how he intended to achieve this.

    "Will pay you back and do the best job”

    Donald Trump

    A symbol of global peace and stability

    A former US official suggested that Trump’s desire to avoid an escalation of the war was partly motivated by the aim of not starting his second presidential term with the conflict worsening. Trump’s spokesperson, Steven Cheung, remarked that Trump had won the election "decisively" and that world leaders were already looking to strengthen ties with him because they saw him as a symbol of global peace and stability.

    In addition to his conversation with Putin, Trump also spoke with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy the day before. Meanwhile, President Joe Biden has invited Trump to the White House for discussions, including a focus on Ukraine and other international issues.

    It's the price you pay

    While the US has provided billions of dollars in aid to Ukraine since Russia’s invasion in February 2022, Trump has often criticised this support, arguing that it is too costly. He also disagrees with the approach of continuing to back Ukraine's efforts to reclaim territories seized by Russia, including Crimea, which Russia annexed in 2014.


    "God spared my life for a reason".

    Donald Trump

    During his call with Putin, the Russian president reportedly made it clear that while he was open to discussing the war, he was unlikely to change Moscow’s demands, which include Ukraine abandoning its NATO ambitions and withdrawing from territories claimed by Russia.


    Additionally, Trump spoke with German Chancellor Olaf Scholz and South Korean President Yoon Suk Yeol in separate calls, reinforcing his commitment to international peace and cooperation. Trump’s approach to foreign policy seems focused on strengthening relationships with allies and maintaining stability, particularly in Europe and the Asia-Pacific region.


    The situation in Ukraine remains tense, with President Zelenskyy continuing to demand increased military support from the West to help defend Ukrainian territory. However, there are also growing calls from some within the US to focus on achieving peace rather than pursuing victory in the conflict.

    Vocabulary List:

    1. Escalate (verb) –  To increase or intensify, especially in the context of a conflict or war.
      • Example: The disturbance escalated into a full-scale riot.
    2. Spokesperson (noun) –  A person who speaks on behalf of another person or organisation.
      • Example: She's a spokesperson for the Scottish Office.
    3. NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organisation) (noun) –  A military alliance of countries from North America and Europe.
      • Example: NATO was set up in 1949, not long after the end of the Second World War.
    4. Aid (noun) –   Help or support, often in the form of money or resources.
      • Example: He saw the pilot slumped in his cockpit and went to his aid.
    5. Annex (verb) –  To take control of a territory, often by force, and incorporate it into one's own country.
      • Example: The first ten amendments were annexed to the Constitution in 1791.
    6. Resolution (noun) –  A formal decision or plan of action.
      • Example: She kept her resolution not to see Anne any more.

    Discussion Questions and Topics:

    1. What did Trump advise Putin regarding the Ukraine war during their phone call?
    2. What was the main reason why Trump’s spokesperson claimed world leaders are eager to work with him?
    3. How did Trump’s conversation with Putin relate to his views on the situation in Ukraine?
    4. What do you think is the most effective way to end the war in Ukraine?
    5. Do you believe that world leaders should prioritise peace or victory in conflicts like the Ukraine war?
    6. How important is the role of the US in international conflicts like the one in Ukraine?
    7. What do you think about the relationship between the US and Russia during Trump’s presidency?
