    Frequently Asked Questions

    1. How do online English classes work?


    Online English classes on Fluent Waves are conducted through video conferencing tools on the platform’s built-in virtual classroom. Classes are one-on-one, and lessons include speaking, listening, reading, and writing activities.

    2. Do I need to have a certain level of English to start?


    No! Fluent waves accept students of all levels, from beginners to advanced learners.

    3. How much do online English classes cost?


    Prices vary depending on the platform, teacher qualifications, and lesson type. Costs range from €15 per hour. At Fluent Waves we offer subscription plans & package deals.

    4. How long does it take to become fluent in English?


    Fluency depends on your starting level, learning consistency, and practise. On average, it takes 6 months to 2 years of regular lessons and practice to achieve conversational fluency.

    5. Can I reschedule or cancel a lesson if I’m unavailable?


    Fluent Waves allow you to reschedule or cancel a lesson. We do require at least 24-hour notice.

    6. What if I have trouble understanding my teacher?


    Your tutor Joan uses simple language, visual aids, and interactive methods to help students understand. If you still struggle, you can request a slower teaching pace.

    7. What materials do I need for online English lessons?


    Generally, you need a stable internet connection, a computer or smartphone, a microphone, and a webcam. Some lessons may require a notebook, but here at Fluent Waves your tutor will type out all of your errors or mistakes during the lesson.

    8. How can I improve my English faster outside of lessons?


    Practise daily by watching English movies, reading books, listening to podcasts, and speaking with native speakers.

    9. Can I learn English online even if I’m shy or not confident?


    Yes! Many platforms offer one-on-one lessons, so you can learn at your own pace in a comfortable environment. Joan is trained to help shy learners build confidence step by step.

    10. What if I don’t see progress in my English skills?


    Progress takes time, but if you feel stuck, try changing your study methods, increasing practise time. Setting clear goals and tracking progress can also help you stay motivated.
